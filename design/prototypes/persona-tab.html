About

Aragorn, son of Arathorn, walks the world under many names — Strider to the folk of Bree, Thorongil to those who knew him in Gondor, and to the Rangers of the North he is simply the Chieftain. He has spent four decades earning the trust of those he protects, never asking for their faith in return, only their patience.

He carries little he does not need. His gear is worn smooth by years of wilderness travel, each piece chosen for function over appearance. He sleeps lightly and wakes early, a habit built across countless nights watching the Greenway for worse things than ill-tempered travelers. To those who look closely there is a weariness in him that has nothing to do with fatigue — the weight of a destiny deferred for good reason.

He is not humorless, though strangers rarely see evidence of it. Among trusted companions he is quietly generous: sharing fire, food, and the careful honesty of someone who has learned that hope is a resource that must be rationed wisely and offered without sentiment.
